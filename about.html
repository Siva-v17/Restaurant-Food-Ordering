Our Story

Nineteen cooks, one hearth, zero shortcuts.

Stackly began as a single wood-fired oven in a parking lot. Nine years later, it's still built around fire — just with a lot more people around the table.

The Journey

How the fire spread.

2016

A Parking Lot Oven

Chef Elena Moss starts serving wood-fired flatbreads from a converted trailer three nights a week.

2018

The Harbor Row Space

Stackly opens its first dining room — 40 seats, one eight-foot hearth, a menu that changes daily.

2021

Whole-Animal Butchery

We bring butchery in-house, partnering with three regional farms for full-animal, nose-to-tail cooking.

2024

Online Ordering & Delivery

Stackly launches contactless ordering and a delivery fleet built to keep smoke flavor intact.

2026

Four James Beard Nods

Recognized regionally for Best Chef and Outstanding Hospitality, four years running.

Our Philosophy

Three rules we don't break.

01 — Fire is the only cooking method.

No sous-vide, no induction. If it can't be touched by wood smoke, it doesn't leave the kitchen.

02 — Every animal, fully used.

Bones become stock, trim becomes sausage, fat becomes the butter on your bread.

03 — The table decides the pace.

Dishes land when they're ready, family style, meant to be shared and lingered over.

Sourcing & Sustainability

Where the fire's fuel comes from.

Farms

Three Family Partners

Every vegetable and cut of meat is traced to one of three farms within 40 miles, visited weekly by our kitchen team.

Wood

Responsibly Harvested

Our oak and applewood come from certified sustainable lots — no old growth, nothing shipped further than it needs to be.

Waste

Nose-to-Tail, Root-to-Stem

Trim becomes stock, peels become pickles, and what's left over goes to a compost program with our farm partners.
